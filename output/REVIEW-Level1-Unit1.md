## 📄 Reading A: Colors and Joy Around the World

Every year, millions of people around the world *celebrate* special festivals that bring color, music, and happiness to their communities. These cultural events help people remember their history and share their traditions with family and friends.

One of the most *colorful* festivals in the world is Holi, the Festival of Colors in India. During this spring celebration, people throw bright colored powders at each other and dance in the streets. The festival is very *popular* with young people, and over 200 million people participate each year. The colors represent the arrival of spring and the victory of good over evil.

In Saudi Arabia, the Janadriyah National Festival is a *wonderful* celebration of Saudi culture and heritage. This annual event features traditional music, poetry, handicrafts, and delicious local food. Families *gather* together to enjoy camel races, falconry displays, and folk dances. The festival *attracts* more than two million visitors from around the kingdom and other countries.

Many festivals around the world share similar elements. People wear *special* clothes, prepare *traditional* foods, and perform cultural dances. These celebrations help younger generations learn about their culture and feel *proud* of their heritage. Festivals also bring tourists to different regions, which helps local businesses and communities grow.

Today, social media helps festivals become known worldwide. People share photos and videos of their celebrations, and this *connects* people from different cultures. Whether it's the lantern festivals in China, harvest celebrations in Europe, or cultural festivals in the Middle East, these events remind us that joy and community are universal human experiences.

## 📄 Reading B: Colors That Tell Stories

Festival clothing around the world is more than just *beautiful* fabric. Every color, pattern, and design tells a story about culture and history. When people wear traditional clothes at festivals, they *celebrate* their identity and share their heritage with others.

In many cultures, specific colors have *special* meanings during festivals. Red often represents good luck and happiness in Chinese New Year celebrations. Women *wear* bright red dresses and decorations to welcome prosperity. In India, people throw colorful powder during Holi festival. The *bright* yellow, pink, and blue colors *represent* joy, love, and new beginnings. Each color has a purpose and meaning.

Festival clothing also shows social connections within communities. Families often *prepare* matching outfits months before big celebrations. Mothers and daughters *spend* hours choosing fabrics and designs together. This preparation time *strengthens* family bonds and passes traditions to younger generations. In Scotland, different clans wear specific tartan patterns during Highland Games. These patterns help people identify their family history and connect with relatives.

Today, festival fashion continues to evolve while keeping traditional elements. Modern designers *combine* ancient patterns with contemporary styles. Young people can honor their culture while expressing personal creativity. Fashion shows now feature traditional festival wear from different countries. This helps people around the world appreciate diverse cultural expressions through clothing.

Festival clothing serves as a bridge between past and present. It helps communities maintain their cultural identity in our globalized world. When someone wears traditional festival dress, they carry forward centuries of stories, beliefs, and artistic traditions. These colorful celebrations remind us that diversity makes our world more interesting and beautiful.

## 📐 Grammar: simple past (regular)

**Explanation (EN):** <b>Simple Past Tense - Regular Verbs</b><br><br>We use the simple past tense to talk about actions that happened and finished in the past.<br><br><b>How to form regular past tense:</b><br>Add <b>-ed</b> to the base form of regular verbs.<br><br><b>Spelling rules:</b><br>• Most verbs: add -ed (walk → walked)<br>• Verbs ending in -e: add -d (dance → danced)<br>• Verbs ending in consonant + y: change y to i + ed (study → studied)<br>• Short verbs ending in consonant-vowel-consonant: double the last...

**Explanation (AR):** الماضي البسيط للأفعال المنتظمة: نضيف -ed للفعل الأساسي للتعبير عن أحداث انتهت في الماضي

**Formula:** Subject + Verb + ed

**Examples:**
- We celebrated Eid with our family last month. → احتفلنا بالعيد مع عائلتنا الشهر الماضي
- She danced at the wedding festival yesterday. → رقصت في حفل الزفاف أمس
- They watched the parade during the national holiday. → شاهدوا المسيرة خلال العطلة الوطنية

**Common Mistakes:**
- ❌ I celebrate Eid yesterday → ✅ I celebrated Eid yesterday (لا تنس إضافة -ed للماضي)
- ❌ She danceed at the party → ✅ She danced at the party (الأفعال المنتهية بـ e نضيف فقط d)
- ❌ We studyed about festivals → ✅ We studied about festivals (الأفعال المنتهية بـ y نحولها إلى ied)
